An AI Pipeline for Automated Assessment of Lumbar Spinal Stenosis Using Multi-Institutional MRI

So Hyeon Lee, Young Jae Kim, Joon Young Jung, Kwang Gi Kim
Gachon University
47th Annual International Conference of the IEEE EMBS (EMBC 2026)
Lab website ORCID LinkedIn

Abstract

Lumbar spinal stenosis requires accurate localization and severity assessment for effective clinical decision-making. We propose an AI pipeline for automated stenotic region localization and severity classification using axial and sagittal MRI images. The system explicitly accounts for anatomical differences between imaging planes by applying plane-specific processing for stenotic region detection and subsequent severity grading. The detected regions of interest are subsequently classified into four severity categories. Experimental results using multi-institutional MRI data demonstrated robust stenotic region localization and stable severity classification performance for both central canal and nerve root canal stenosis.

Method

We developed a two-stage, view-aware AI pipeline for automated lumbar spinal stenosis assessment.

  • Step 1. MRI view classification (Axial vs. Sagittal)
  • Step 2. View-specific anatomical region detection
    • Central canal (Axial)
    • Nerve root canal (Sagittal)
  • Step 3. Pixel spacing-based ROI normalization
  • Step 4. Stenosis severity classification using dedicated models

Figure 1. Overview of the proposed AI pipeline for automated lumbar spinal stenosis assessment.

Introduction

Lumbar spinal stenosis is a common degenerative spinal disorder characterized by narrowing of the spinal canal or nerve root canal. MRI plays a central role in evaluating the anatomical location and severity of stenosis.

However, manual MRI assessment requires the interpretation of multiple imaging views and anatomical regions and may be affected by inter-observer variability. Therefore, an automated and standardized assessment method could support more efficient and consistent evaluation.
